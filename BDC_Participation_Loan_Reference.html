BDC Business Elevation Loan

A quick reference covering how BDC Participation Loans are structured in FaaSBank, why certain fields, options, and restrictions appear when you set one up, and what those rules mean for your borrower's repayment schedule.

1 · Overview

What is a BDC Business Elevation Loan?

A loan co-funded 50/50 by your Community Futures organization (CF) and the Business Development Bank of Canada (BDC).

The borrower sees one loan, one interest rate, and one payment. Behind the scenes, FaaSBank maintains two funds, two balances, and separate interest accruals so each side can be reported on independently.

Fees, insurance, and other non-interest items remain 100% with the CF. Only principal and interest split with BDC.

Created in FaaSBank by ticking the Multi? checkbox when adding a new loan. This opens the BDC Participation Loan setup modal.

Participation Split
CF 50%
BDC 50%

Each side has its own loan fund, balance, interest ledger, and GL postings — but the borrower only ever sees the combined view.

2 · Rate Structure

How rates are set and what each party earns

  • Rates are fixed at origination, not pegged to Prime. Prime is used only to set the BDC floor at the time of origination.
  • BDC's portion always earns at least Prime + 4% — this is the BDC floor rate.
  • You enter the Borrower Fixed Rate (%) in the BDC Participation Loan modal. FaaSBank derives CF Fixed Rate (%) and BDC Fixed Rate (%) from that.

Example with Prime = 4.45% ⇒ BDC floor = 8.45%. A CF rate of 6.45% pairs with the 8.45% floor to give the borrower 7.45% — the average of the two sides.

3 · Centrepiece

Straight-Line vs. Blended repayment

Two repayment structures are supported, selected via the Repayment Type field in the FaaSBank Calculator. Most program rules — day count, insurance eligibility, partial-disbursement behaviour — flow from this single choice.

Repayment Types Set via the Repayment Type field in the Calculator
  Straight-Line Blended
Available when Always Only when Borrower Rate = BDC floor
Borrower payment structure Constant principal each month; total payment decreases over time as the interest portion shrinks. Constant total payment each month for the life of the loan; the P/I split rebalances internally.
Day Count Actual/Actual — interest based on the actual days in each period. 30/360 — every month treated as 30 days; year as 360 days.
Insurance Permitted Not permitted (BDC program rule)
During partial disbursement Principal repayments can begin immediately, but have the option of interest only for 12 months. Total payment fluctuates as interest tracks the advanced balance. Interest-only payments until the loan is fully disbursed; principal payments begin only after final disbursement. Note: you are allowed to have 12 months interest-only.
Changing repayment type later
Repayment type or day count can be changed mid-life with BDC's agreement. The CF documents the change with the borrower, informs BDC, and the new structure is applied in FaaSBank by creating a Bring Forward amortization schedule effective from the agreed date. Changes apply prospectively only — never retroactively.

4 · Insurance

Allowed on Straight-Line only

Insurance is permitted only on Straight-Line loans. On Blended loans, FaaSBank automatically disables the insurance fields in the Calculator and surfaces this message:

FaaSBank message
BDC program requirement: Insurance is permitted only on Straight-Line repayment loans.

Why: Blended payments require an exact 50/50 P&I split on every payment so each side accrues equally. Declining-balance insurance changes the principal portion month-to-month, which breaks that equality. It's a BDC program rule, not a FaaSBank limitation.

5 · Payments

When payments are due and how to post them

Schedule

Payments are always due on the 1st of the month. The CF collects from the borrower on the 1st; BDC collects from the CF on the 15th. Frequency is fixed — monthly only.

First payment date

The Initial Payment Date is auto-set from the Disbursal Date:

  • Disbursed on/before the cutoff (6 business days before the upcoming 1st) → first payment is that 1st.
  • Disbursed after the cutoff → first payment rolls to the 1st of the following month.

Statutory holidays aren't factored in — if one falls in the window, adjust the date manually.

Early & late payments

Early payments must be posted as a principal-only / manual payment, with no penalty. FaaSBank's default applies interest first, so it will prompt you in the payment workflow when a BDC loan is involved.

Late payments post normally — interest accrues to the actual posting date, and BDC reconciles any split differences on their side.

6 · Disbursement & Origination

Disbursement windows and what you can't do

  • Loans must be fully disbursed within 120 days of the Loan Agreement. Any undisbursed portion lapses and is cancelled. This is a program rule monitored by the CF — FaaSBank does not enforce it automatically.
  • Partial disbursements are supported. The behaviour during the partial-disbursement period differs by repayment type — see Section 3.
  • Refinancing an existing CF loan into a BDC participation loan is not permitted. FaaSBank blocks this in the refinance workflow.
  • Top-ups are not allowed. Additional funding requests must be originated as a new, separate loan — not as an extension of the existing one.

7 · Reporting

Seeing the per-fund split

In the loan's Statement tab, expand any transaction row to see how it was split between the CF and BDC funds. Each sub-row shows:

Fund · Amount · Principal · Interest · Closing Principal.

Use this view to reconcile against BDC's records or to answer a borrower or auditor question about what each party received on a given payment.
